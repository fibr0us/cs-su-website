Programme Overview

The Highest Academic
Achievement in Computing

The Doctor of Philosophy in Computer Science at Stellenbosch University represents the highest academic qualification in the field. PhD candidates undertake independent research under expert supervision, making a significant and original contribution to computer science knowledge.

Our doctoral graduates are sought after globally in academia, research labs, and senior industry positions — recognised for their deep expertise, independent thinking, and capacity to drive innovation at the frontier of computing.

PhD Research Journey
1
Phase 1
Proposal & Literature Review
Develop a formal research proposal, conduct a comprehensive literature survey, and define the scope and contribution of your doctoral research.
2
Phase 2
Core Research & Experimentation
Execute the research plan — design experiments, develop theoretical frameworks, collect data, and iterate with supervisor guidance.
3
Phase 3
Writing, Peer Review & Publications
Prepare conference papers and journal articles. Receive peer review feedback. Begin drafting the dissertation manuscript.
4
Phase 4
Submission & Defense
Submit the completed dissertation for external examination and defend your research before an expert committee.

Admission Criteria

Requirements

What you need to apply for the PhD programme

🎓 Academic Requirements
  • Master's degree in Computer Science or a closely related field
  • Strong research aptitude demonstrated through your Master's thesis or equivalent
  • An identified supervisor willing to take you on as a doctoral student
  • An approved research proposal that identifies a gap and original contribution
📋 Application Documents
  • Certified academic transcripts from all previous degrees
  • Research proposal (minimum 5 pages) including problem statement and methodology
  • Curriculum vitae, including any publications or presentations
  • Two academic references from supervisors or senior academics
  • English language proficiency proof (where applicable for international students)
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Limited part-time options exist for the PhD. Full-time study is strongly recommended to maintain research momentum and meet the degree's demands. If you require a part-time arrangement, discuss this directly with your potential supervisor before applying — any accommodation is subject to their approval and departmental policy.

You can apply before securing full funding. Several sources become available once you have received an offer: NRF bursaries, SU Postgraduate Merit Bursaries, and supervisor-linked research chair funding. Your supervisor may also have access to project funds. We strongly encourage all applicants to apply for NRF funding simultaneously with their admission application.

Most students complete their PhD in 3 to 4 years. The timeline varies with research area, problem complexity, and publication requirements. Theoretical CS topics may finish closer to 3 years, while experimental or systems work often takes longer. Your supervisor will help you set realistic milestones from the outset.

Yes, Stellenbosch University warmly welcomes international PhD students. The application process is the same, with the addition of certified English translations of non-English transcripts and English proficiency documentation where required. The SU International Office provides support for visa and residency processes once an offer has been made.
