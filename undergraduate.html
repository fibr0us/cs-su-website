🎓
BSc Computer Science
⏱ 3 Years 📋 NQF Level 7 🏛 Faculty of Science

The flagship undergraduate programme in the Department of Computer Science at Stellenbosch University. Grounded in theoretical foundations and hands-on programming, this degree equips graduates with the analytical thinking and technical skills demanded by industry, academia, and innovation-driven careers. Students choose from five focal areas, tailoring their degree to their interests.

Entry Requirements

  • Mathematics — at least 70% in Matric (NSC)
  • Physical Sciences recommended
  • National Senior Certificate or equivalent
  • English proficiency (NSC or equivalent)

BDatSci — Computer Science
⏱ 4 Years 📋 NQF Level 8 🤝 Interfaculty

An interfaculty programme offered jointly by the Faculties of Science and Economic & Management Sciences. The Computer Science focal area contains the complete CS curriculum alongside modules from Statistics and Data Science, producing graduates who are equally fluent in algorithms and quantitative methods.

BDatSci is a 4-year degree at NQF Level 8, one level above the BSc — equivalent to an honours degree on entry into the workforce.

Getting Started

First-Year CS Modules

Your journey in Computer Science begins here. These foundational modules build the programming fluency and conceptual thinking you'll use throughout your degree — and your career.

CS113
Semester 1
Computer Science for Actuarial Studies

Financial programming, spreadsheet automation, and introductory programming tailored to actuarial and quantitative finance contexts. Bridges the gap between mathematical models and computational implementation.

CS114
Semester 1
Introductory Computer Science 1

The gateway module for all CS students. Covers basic programming constructs, data representation, arrays, recursion, and an introduction to object-oriented programming. Develops computational thinking from the ground up.

CS144
Semester 2
Introductory Computer Science 2

Building directly on CS114, this module introduces fundamental data structures, algorithm design, complexity theory, and advanced object-oriented programming concepts including design patterns and software architecture.
