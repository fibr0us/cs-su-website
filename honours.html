Programme Overview

One year. Serious depth.

The BCmp Honours programme is a one-year postgraduate qualification designed for students who have completed an undergraduate Computer Science degree. The programme combines advanced coursework with a substantial research project, preparing graduates for research careers or advanced industry roles.

Advanced Algorithms

Advanced Topics I: Vulnerability Discovery and Exploitation

Modern techniques for discovering software vulnerabilities including static analysis, fuzzing, symbolic execution, and responsible disclosure.

Advanced Topics II: Ontology Engineering

Design and implementation of ontologies for the Semantic Web and knowledge representation systems. OWL, SPARQL, and ontology reasoning.

CS 771 — Project 32 Credits

Honours Project 771

Supervisor: TBA (assigned based on research interests)

Independent research project under faculty supervision, culminating in a written thesis and oral presentation. Students are expected to make an original contribution to their chosen field.

Additional modules from the postgraduate module pool may be announced each year. Students select 6 modules in total; the project is compulsory. Module availability subject to change.
Admissions

Application Process

Four steps to joining the programme.

1

Check Eligibility

You need a BSc in Computer Science or an equivalent qualification at NQF Level 7, with a minimum 60% average in your final year. Contact us if unsure about equivalency.

2

Find a Supervisor

Before applying, identify a potential supervisor whose research interests align with yours. Browse staff profiles and reach out via postgrad@cs.sun.ac.za.

3

Submit Application

Complete your application through Stellenbosch University's official online application portal. Include your academic transcripts, CV, and motivation letter.

4

Accept Offer

Upon receiving an offer of admission, confirm your enrollment before the stated deadline. Late confirmation may forfeit your place.
