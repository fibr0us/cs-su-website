Our Division

About the Division

Founded in 1972, the Computer Science Division is a proud part of the Department of Mathematical Sciences at Stellenbosch University. Situated in the heart of one of South Africa's most beautiful university towns, we are home to 11 full academic staff members and a vibrant community of postgraduate researchers.

Our division bridges the gap between rigorous theoretical foundations and cutting-edge applied research, producing graduates who lead in industry, academia, and public service across South Africa and beyond.

Research Excellence — Four active research groups spanning Automata Theory, Software Engineering, Machine Learning, and Computer Networks.

Comprehensive Curriculum — From foundational BSc to doctoral research, with strong industry connections and interfaculty programmes.

Located in Matieland — Stellenbosch campus offers world-class facilities in a collaborative and inspiring environment.

Research Output

Selected Publications

Recent peer-reviewed contributions from our research groups — spanning theory, AI, software engineering, and networks.

Machine Learning 2025

Scalable Reinforcement Learning for Resource-Constrained Environments

S. Kroon, M. Ngxande — NeurIPS 2025

Novel policy-gradient methods that reduce sample complexity by 40%, enabling deployment on edge devices for real-time decision-making in agricultural monitoring systems.

Software Verification 2025

Automated Protocol Verification with Compositional Model Checking

B. Fischer, W. Visser — ICSE 2025

A compositional approach to verifying complex distributed protocols, achieving order-of-magnitude speedups over monolithic methods while maintaining completeness guarantees.

Formal Methods 2025

Minimal NFA Constructions for Pattern Matching with Bounded Delay

L. van Zijl, A.B. van der Merwe — Theoretical Computer Science, Vol. 987

New constructions for bounded-delay NFAs that are provably minimal, with applications to streaming pattern matching and real-time text processing systems.

Computer Vision 2024

Deep Satellite Image Segmentation for Precision Agriculture in Southern Africa

T.L. Grobler, M. Ngxande — Remote Sensing, 16(4)

Transfer learning techniques applied to Sentinel-2 imagery for automated crop classification and yield estimation, validated across multiple South African biomes.

Swarm Intelligence 2024

Particle Swarm Optimization for Large-Scale Neural Architecture Search

A.P. Engelbrecht — IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation, 28(3)

Multi-objective PSO variants that co-optimize accuracy and computational cost in neural architecture search, outperforming gradient-based NAS methods on standard benchmarks.

HCI & Accessibility 2024

Accessible Mobile Interfaces for Low-Literacy Users in Resource-Limited Settings

B. Tucker — ACM CHI 2024

Co-designed mobile health interfaces for rural South African communities, demonstrating 65% improvement in task completion for users with limited digital literacy.
