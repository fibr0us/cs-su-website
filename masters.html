Programme Overview

Advance the
Boundaries of Knowledge

The Master's programme at Stellenbosch University's Computer Science Division is a research-intensive two-year qualification. Students work closely with a faculty supervisor to produce original research in their chosen area, culminating in a substantial written thesis.

Our graduates pursue careers in industry R&D, academia, and technology leadership — equipped with deep domain expertise and the analytical rigour demanded by the world's foremost research organisations.

2-Year Research Journey
1
Months 1 – 6
Foundation & Proposal
In-depth literature review, identifying research gaps, and developing a formal research proposal with your supervisor.
2
Months 7 – 12
Core Research & Experiments
Design and execute experiments, develop prototypes or theoretical frameworks, collect and analyse results.
3
Year 2
Writing & Submission
Synthesise findings, write the thesis manuscript, incorporate supervisor feedback, and submit for examination.

How to Apply

Application Process

Follow these steps to begin your Master's journey at Stellenbosch

1
Identify a Potential Supervisor
Browse the research areas and supervisor profiles above. Find faculty whose work aligns with your research interests and background.
2
Contact Them Directly
Reach out by email to discuss your research interests and proposed ideas. Include a brief academic background and a short description of your concept.
3
Apply via the SU Online Portal
Submit your formal application through the Stellenbosch University online admissions portal. Applications open annually — check the SU website for current deadlines.
4
Submit Required Documents
Provide: certified academic transcripts, an up-to-date CV, a research proposal (2–3 pages), and two academic references who can speak to your research capability.
5
Receive Decision & Enrol
The admissions committee will review your application. Upon acceptance, you will receive an offer and can complete your enrolment to begin your programme.
Financial Support

Funding Opportunities

A range of funding options exist to support your postgraduate studies

🏛️
National Research Foundation (NRF)
The NRF offers competitive bursaries for postgraduate students in science, engineering, and technology. Apply via the NRF online system in the year preceding your studies.
🎓
SU Postgraduate Merit Bursary
Stellenbosch University awards merit bursaries to high-achieving postgraduate students. These are awarded based on academic excellence and are application-based.
🔬
Research Chair Funding
Several faculty hold DSI/NRF Research Chairs with dedicated funding for postgraduate students working within their research groups and projects.
🤝
Industry Partnerships
Sponsored research projects with industry partners may include student bursaries. Discuss available opportunities with your potential supervisor during initial contact.
