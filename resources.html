Section 01

Academic Support

We're committed to your success. Multiple support channels are available throughout the semester.

Tutors & Consultants

First-year and second-year modules have dedicated tutors. Check SUNLearn for your module's tutor schedule and consultation times.

Writing Centre

The SU Writing Centre offers help with academic writing, reports, and theses. Especially valuable for Honours and postgraduate students.

Mathematics Support

The Mathematics Division offers academic support for struggling students in quantitative and mathematical modules relevant to CS.

CS Staff Office Hours

Most CS staff keep regular office hours. Check individual staff pages on the department website, or contact them directly by email.

Section 02

Computing Resources

Access the hardware, software, and infrastructure you need for your studies and research.

Computer Labs

The Department has dedicated computer labs available to CS students throughout the semester. Access requires a valid student card.

Linux Systems

CS students have access to departmental Linux servers for coursework and research tasks. SSH access is provided after enrolment.

Software Licences

SU provides licences for Microsoft Office, MATLAB, and other commercial tools via IT Services. Visit its.sun.ac.za for the full software catalogue.

Cloud Computing

Research students may apply for access to cloud computing resources for ML and data-intensive workloads. Contact your supervisor to apply.

Directions by Car

From the N1/N2, take the R304 towards Stellenbosch. Follow Victoria Street, then turn onto Merriman Avenue. The Mathematical Sciences Building (A-wing) is on the main campus, accessible via Bosman Street. Visitor parking is available on Merriman Avenue and at the Neelsie Student Centre parking garage.

Public Transport & Parking

The campus is a 15-minute walk from Stellenbosch train station. MyCiTi and Golden Arrow bus routes serve the town centre. On-campus pay parking is available at the Neelsie Centre garage and along Merriman Avenue. Bicycle racks are located outside the Mathematical Sciences Building.

Section 05

Postgraduate Resources

Dedicated information for Honours, Master's, and PhD students in the CS Division.

NRF Bursary Applications

South African postgraduate students can apply for NRF bursaries and scholarships. Applications typically open in April–May for the following year. Visit nrf.ac.za for current calls.

Thesis Formatting Guidelines

Stellenbosch University provides official formatting guidelines for dissertations and theses. Download the template from the SU Library website or contact your faculty administrator.

Ethics Clearance

Research involving human subjects, surveys, or sensitive data requires ethics committee clearance. Contact the Faculty Research Ethics Committee early — the process can take 4–8 weeks.

Conference Funding (NRF)

Registered postgraduate students may apply for NRF conference funding to present their research at national and international conferences. Consult your supervisor and the postgraduate office.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common student queries.

Module registration is done via the SU Student Portal (mystudies.sun.ac.za). Consult your programme advisor if you need assistance selecting modules or if a prerequisite approval is required. Registration windows open before each semester — check the academic calendar.
Contact the Faculty of Science's student administration office for queries about your academic record, transcripts, or re-marks. Your academic record can also be viewed via the SU Student Portal.
Browse staff research interests on the department website, then email potential supervisors directly with a brief motivation and your academic record. Contact postgrad@cs.sun.ac.za for guidance if you're unsure where to start. It is advisable to approach supervisors early, as capacity is limited.
Undergraduate programmes (BSc CS, BDatSci) are generally full-time. Some postgraduate options — particularly Master's by research — may accommodate part-time arrangements depending on supervisor agreement. Contact postgrad@cs.sun.ac.za for specific queries.
Linux server access is set up as part of module onboarding in relevant modules (e.g., SC272). Your lecturer or tutor will provide SSH credentials and connection details at the start of the semester. Contact secretary@cs.sun.ac.za for general IT access queries.
The Honours project (CSc781) officially begins with the Honours orientation at the start of February. The project registration deadline is 6 February, and Honours lectures begin 9 February. Students are strongly encouraged to identify a supervisor and preliminary topic before the start of the year.
